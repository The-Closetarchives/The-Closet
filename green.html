The Green Children of Woolpit.

The legend of the Green Children of Woolpit emerged in the mid-12th century, during a period of civil war and political unrest in England known as The Anarchy.It was during the reign of King Stephen that villagers in Woolpit reportedly encountered two mysterious children with green skin and an unknown language.

The Children: Discovery

During the mid-12th century, in the small village of Woolpit, Suffolk, England. Woolpit, pronounced as Wulfpytt is an ancient village in Suffolk as the name refers a pit for catching wolves. Now, the villagers encountered two children around the pit. At the first glance they seemed ordinary but the villagers instantly caught some strange features that set them apart from everyone else. The villagers came across two young children with green skin, talking gibberish and acting nervous.

One of earliest written account of the Green Children comes from Ralph of Coggeshall, an English chronicler who wrote about these children in his writings. He writes,the children were taken to a nearby home of Sir Richard de Calne where he provided them food but they refused to eat anything. This continued for days until the children found some green beans from Richard's garden which they ate straight out of ground.
